Understanding Wagering Requirements

The single most important concept to grasp is the wagering requirement, also known as a playthrough or turnover requirement. This is a multiplier that dictates the total amount of money you must bet before your bonus funds (and any winnings derived from them) are converted into your real money balance and become eligible for withdrawal.

For example, let's say you receive a $10 complimentary bonus with a 45x wagering requirement. This means you must place a total of $450 in bets ($10 x 45) before that initial $10 and any winnings from it are unlocked. It doesn't mean you have to lose $450; it's simply the total volume of wagers you must make. Every bet you place contributes to this total, regardless of whether you win or lose the bet.

Game Contributions and Restrictions

A crucial addendum to wagering requirements is the concept of game contributions. Not all games are created equal when it comes to clearing a bonus. The percentage that a bet on a certain game contributes to the wagering requirement is known as its "game weighting."

  • Slots: Almost universally, bets on slot machines contribute 100% to the wagering requirement. This makes them the most efficient way to clear a bonus.
  • Table Games: Games like Blackjack, Roulette, and Baccarat have a much lower house edge, meaning the player has a better statistical chance. Because of this, bets on these games typically contribute a much smaller percentage, often only 5% or 10%.
  • Live Casino and Other Games: Live dealer games, video poker, and certain other specialty games may contribute an even smaller percentage or, in some cases, 0%.

Always check the bonus terms for a detailed list of game contribution percentages.
